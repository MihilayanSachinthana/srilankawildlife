Exploring wildlife

Introduction

Sri Lanka, known as the "Pearl of the Indian Ocean," is a
tropical paradise with diverse wildlife. From iconic elephants
to elusive leopards, its national parks like Yala and Wilpattu
are crucial for conservation.

The island's lush landscapes echo with the sounds of a vibrant
ecosystem, while its oceans teem with marine life. Beyond
ecological importance, Sri Lanka's wildlife holds cultural
significance, woven into indigenous beliefs and folk tales.
The nation's commitment to conservation ensures the
preservation of this extraordinary natural heritage.
